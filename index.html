01 Frame the meeting

What we're trying to leave aligned on.

This is a concept-design brainstorm, not a presentation. We are not asking the manager to approve a deliverable — we are asking for a reaction to a shape for the broader CX strategic-outlook project: how we frame the market, how we let the board feel time pass, and what kind of artifact we ultimately put in front of them.

We want to leave the room aligned on three things only: (1) the right archetype set to reason about, (2) the right pair of axes to organize them, and (3) whether the strategic scenario sandbox is the right centerpiece for the outlook. Everything else is downstream.

The horizon is end-2028 — about thirty months out. Far enough to let structural shifts play through; near enough to stay disciplined about which named events actually matter. Anything past 2028 stops being strategy and starts being futurism.

The board audience is educational, not decision-forcing. Success is measured in mental-model change at thirty days, not a recommendation the directors sign off on. A static 2×2 freezes the mental model on the day we draw it; a sandbox lets directors feel which futures we believe are possible and why.

02 The archetype set

Five archetypes, locked.

The competitive map collapses to five structurally distinct positions. CPaaS and BPOs sit explicitly off the 2×2 — they appear in the broader narrative as infrastructure and delivery layers below the map, not peer archetypes.

Archetype 01

Foundational AI

OpenAI · Anthropic · Google Gemini

Foundation models + agent runtime as platform; selectively ship reference apps; own the "front door" via consumer + Workspace surface. Captures upside via per-token revenue and selective vertical entry.

Archetype 02

AI-native agents

Sierra · Decagon

Autonomous-resolution agents priced on outcome / consumption. Design-language differentiation (Sierra Agent OS, Decagon AOPs); model-agnostic on top of foundational LLMs; high-touch implementation in regulated verticals.

Archetype 03

Hyperscalers

AWS · Microsoft

Consumption-priced contact-center on cloud infrastructure with first-party LLM (Q, Gemini) and bring-your-own-CRM. Microsoft folds here rather than into workflow platforms — hyperscaler infra and the Azure-AI-Foundry hedge are the anchor.

Archetype 04

Pure-play CCaaS

NICE · Genesys

Cloud contact-center suites (routing, omnichannel, WFM, QM, agent AI) sold to enterprises. Per-seat shifting to consumption + AI add-on pricing; own orchestration, rent voice from CPaaS. Betting on outcome pricing + vertical AI.

Archetype 05

Workflow platforms

Salesforce · ServiceNow

CRM and workflow as the system of record + system of action, with contact center bundled as add-on (Service Cloud, Customer Service, Now Platform). Consumption-credit pricing — Flex Credits in Salesforce's case.

Three flagged additions for the brainstorm to react to.

Not in the v1 set, but worth raising before we lock.
Open-source / sovereign AILlama · Mistral · DeepSeek · sovereign deployments

Real if the EU AI Act and data-residency dynamics push enterprises off US frontier models. Could justify a sixth archetype if the brainstorm believes sovereignty pressure is structural rather than cyclical.

Vertical-AI specialistsHippocratic · Kasisto · Abridge

If "vertical depth wins" is one of the futures we want to pressure-test, this is the archetype that carries the thesis. Healthcare, banking, clinical documentation each have credible specialist leaders.

Customer-side in-house AIGoldman Sachs · JPMorgan · enterprise internal teams

Doesn't move any vendor on the map — it removes demand from every vendor category at once. Worth flagging as the structural-break pressure rather than a competitive position.

03 Axis options

Two pairs to bring to the brainstorm; one to flag as rejected.

The 2×2 is a thinking frame, not a deliverable frame. We bring the recommended axes plus one workshopping alternative; the manager picks the one that feels right when projected on the wall.

Primary recommendation

Workflow ownership × AI execution depth.

Strategic-groups classic. The cleanest map for the events we'd actually script — Salesforce-acquires-Genesys moves Salesforce up-right, OpenAI-ships-CS-agent moves OpenAI up, foundational-model-price-collapse compresses the AI-native cluster's margin moat without moving the dot.

Top-right"The dream" — empty today
Top-leftAI-natives + Foundational AI
Bottom-rightLegacy CRM positioning
Bottom-leftInfrastructure / delivery
Workshopping alternative

Vertical depth × horizontal reach.

Strongest if "vertical AI wins" is a future we want to pressure-test seriously. Useful as a second view of the same archetypes through a different lens — NICE ships an FS bundle (up-right), Hippocratic hits enterprise scale (vertical specialists become a presence on the map at all). Less useful as the primary frame.

Considered, rejected

Model layer × application layer.

Application-layer players (Salesforce, NICE, Genesys, Sierra) are not going to develop their own foundational models in the next 24–36 months. Only hyperscalers and Microsoft would actually move on this axis, which makes it visually static and analytically thin over our horizon. Worth mentioning so the manager knows we considered it.

04 Event taxonomy

Three tiers, ten categories.

The timeline below the 2×2 isn't undifferentiated. It has structure — discrete events that fire on specific dates, background trends that shade across time, and rare-but-named tail-risk shocks.

Tier 1 · Discrete

Point markers on the timeline.

Dated events that fire and change positioning.
  1. M&A / capital events — acquisitions, IPOs, major investments, distressed sales, take-privates.
  2. Product / strategic releases — specific named products from named vendors.
  3. Regulatory enforcement — dated regulatory cliffs, federal and state law passages, major court decisions.
Tier 2 · Background

Shaded bands across time.

Trends and conditions that pressure the map continuously.
  1. Technology trajectories — bot-to-bot interactions, voice-first UX, agent-native interfaces, multi-modal as default, ambient AI, memory-as-substrate.
  2. Pricing model evolution — per-seat → consumption → outcome.
  3. Talent / labor dynamics — BPO erosion, agent-engineering as a market, AI-talent comp arms race.
  4. Capital market conditions — rates, IPO windows, valuation cycles, sovereign AI funding.
  5. Customer behavior shifts — vendor consolidation, AI-native UX preference, outcome-orientation in procurement.
  6. Geopolitical / sovereignty — US-China decoupling, EU data localization, export controls.
Tier 3 · Shock

Rare but named.

Tail-risk events that re-price the entire map.
  1. Crisis events — foundational-lab safety incident, major AI-driven data breach, high-profile autonomous-agent failure, geopolitical shock.

The bot-to-bot point in Tier 2 is special: if my bot calls your bot, every CSAT / AHT / containment metric becomes obsolete at once. That doesn't move a dot — it changes what the axes mean. Real design question for v2.
